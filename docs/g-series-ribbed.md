## Structural Design Overview

G Series inflatable boat fenders are characterized by a ribbed outer surface structure.  
The rib reinforcement increases surface friction while enhancing structural rigidity under compression.

Unlike smooth cylindrical fenders, the ribbed design improves hull grip during dynamic docking situations.

---

## Key Technical Advantages

- Ribbed exterior for anti-slip performance
- Enhanced compression stability
- Improved abrasion resistance
- Compact profile for small craft

The ribbed pattern distributes localized stress and helps reduce rotational movement during impact.

---

## Ideal Applications

G Series fenders are commonly used for:

- Small recreational boats
- Fishing boats
- Dinghies
- RIBs (Rigid Inflatable Boats)
- Jet skis and small craft

They perform particularly well in environments where docking involves repeated short-duration impacts.

---

## Structural Comparison: G Series vs Smooth Cylindrical Designs

| Feature | G Series (Ribbed) | Smooth Cylindrical |
|----------|------------------|--------------------|
| Surface Grip | High | Moderate |
| Abrasion Resistance | Enhanced | Standard |
| Docking Stability | Improved | Basic |
| Ideal Vessel Size | Small to Medium | Medium to Large |

The ribbed design is especially valuable in smaller vessels where surface contact area is limited.
